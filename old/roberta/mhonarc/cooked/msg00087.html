Today is my birthday.  Happy birthday to me!  To celebrate, the weather
is PERFECT (a rare thing indeed in Dunedin), and I've been with Anne to
visit the little penguin we rescued from Sandfly Bay on Saturday night. 
It turns out that SHE is not a Fiordland Crested Penguin, as we thought,
but instead comes from a tiny group of subantarctic islands a whole lot
farther away.  Snares Islands.  There are about 30 000 Snares Island
penguins left in the world, but because they (typically) only inhabit
that one little cluster of rocks in the sea, they are listed as
vulnerable by the WWF.  Who knows how she came to be on our beach, but
if we hadn't picked her up there would definitely be one less of her
kind in the world.  

The very kind keeper at the penguin hospital let us have a good long
look while telling us about their operations.  You can see pictures on
my blog (www.robertajenkins.blogspot.com).  She is still very
underweight, but is doing great and should be fine to return home once
she's been fed more.  She's already gotten through most of her moult,
and looks like an entirely different bird than the one we picked up - no
more patchy, featherless splotches or listlessness.  Now she's sleek and
shiny and black, and wandering around the enclosure on her own.  She
even has a little friend, another Snares Island penguin brought in
recently by someone else!

So this story has a very happy ending.  And a better birthday present
would be hard to find!
